About Ugadi

❈

The dawn of a new year, the beginning of a new chapter

Telugu Ugadi ఉగాది

Ugadi is the Telugu New Year, celebrated on the first day of Chaitra month in the Hindu lunisolar calendar. The word "Ugadi" comes from "Yuga" (era) + "Adi" (beginning) — meaning the start of a new era.

Homes are decorated with mango leaves, rangoli is drawn at the entrance, and families gather to listen to the Panchanga Sravanam — the astrological forecast for the year ahead. The celebration is marked by the unique Ugadi Pachadi — a mixture of six tastes.

Kannada Yugadi ಯುಗಾದಿ

In Karnataka, the same festival is celebrated as Yugadi — the Kannada New Year. It holds equal cultural significance and is celebrated with great enthusiasm across the state.

Bevu-Bella (neem flowers + jaggery) is consumed to symbolize the equal acceptance of bitterness and sweetness in life. The day begins with an oil bath, new clothes, prayers, and the reading of the Panchanga by the family priest.

Though our traditions carry different names — Ugadi & Yugadi — the spirit is the same: gratitude for the past, hope for the future, and joy in the present. We celebrate together as one community.

Ugadi Pachadi

❈

Six tastes. Six emotions. One life.

Ugadi Pachadi is the soul of the festival — a single bowl that holds all six tastes, reminding us that life is a beautiful, bittersweet journey.

Veppaku / Bevu

Bitter

Neem flowers — symbol of Sadness. Life brings sorrow; accept it with grace.

వేప పూవు · ಬೇವು

Bellam / Bella

Sweet

Jaggery — symbol of Happiness. Savour every moment of joy.

బెల్లం · ಬೆಲ್ಲ

Karam / Khara

Spicy

Green chilli — symbol of Challenges. Face them with courage.

కారం · ಖಾರ

Uppu / Uppu

Salty

Salt — symbol of Fear. Acknowledge your fears, they make you human.

ఉప్పు · ಉಪ್ಪು

Chintapandu / Hunase

Sour

Tamarind — symbol of Disgust. Even unpleasant times pass.

చింతపండు · ಹುಣಸೆ

Mamidikaya / Mavina

Tangy

Raw mango — symbol of Surprise. Life's unexpected gifts are precious.
